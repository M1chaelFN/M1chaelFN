DIGITAL FOOTPRINT

WHAT IS THE DIGITAL FOOTPRINT?

Digital Footprint is when use the Internet to connect to social networks or browse search engines, it is as if we were wearing shoes with paint-stained soles. As we walk, we would leave a trail of all the places we have been, if we have stayed there for a longer or shorter time, to which we usually return because we like them more, etc. That ground on which we move is the Internet and the mark of our steps is the digital footprint that we inevitably leave.

TIPS FOR TAKING CARE OF YOUR DIGITAL FOOTPRINT

If hackers access an email account you no longer use, they can view your contacts, impersonate you credibly, search for personal information, and try the same password on other accounts. If you don't use the email address regularly, it could take a while to notice, leaving even more opportunities for damage to occur.
For many people, completely deleting social media accounts is not practical, or even preferable. Instead, minimize both your exposure and the data you share with others by limiting posts to “friends only,” turning off location data collection, and clicking the options to remove your profile from results on public search engines. However, if there are accounts that you no longer use, deactivate them.
Create a specific account for marketing and other promotions, such as store discounts, and limit any identifying information you store in that email account. That way, if a marketing database is breached and your account is compromised, you can simply delete it to try to minimize the loss of digital information. (Bonus: This can help dramatically reduce the amount of spam in your "real" email.)
With a few clicks — and usually a few dollars — anyone can learn the names, addresses, phone numbers and other data of millions of people through online data search engines (a often called "people search" sites. And if you become the target of malicious online trolls, they can quickly spread this information, which could result in continued harassment. When ordering online or signing up for newsletters or other communications, click the "opt-out" button so the sites cannot share your information with marketing database companies. Additionally, you can work with online data searchers to opt out. Browse "online data finders" for a list. Each has different steps — including letters, faxes, or other formal requests — to remove you, but it may be worth the effort.

DRAWBACKS

MAY CAUSE PRIVACY CONCERNS
Some people may be concerned about how much information is collected through fingerprinting and how it is used. This can lead to a lack of trust in online privacy.
CAN BE USED FOR MALICIOUS PURPOSES
The information collected through fingerprinting can also be used by people with bad intentions, such as hackers and other cybercriminals, to carry out attacks and steal personal information.
CAN LEAD TO ADVERTISING OVERSATURATION
If the use of digital fingerprinting to deliver personalized ads is abused, it can result in advertising oversaturation that can be annoying for users.
